# Regional Source Overlays: EU and MENA

The intelligence categories remain the same across markets.

The sources do not.

This is the companion overlay system for the [Competitive Research Compendium](competitive-research-compendium.md). Use it after completing the compendium's **Instantiate for Any Engagement** block and identifying the target [GEOGRAPHY]. It adds regional sources, disclosure environments, and signals without duplicating the core disciplines (FININT, TECHINT, GEOINT/DEMOINT, and the rest) — procurement and regulatory sources map onto FININT and MASINT respectively.

## European Union Source Overlay

The EU offers unusually rich public data, but it is spread across EU-level portals, national registries, regulators, standards bodies, and individual Member State procurement systems.

### EU Procurement and Contract Intelligence

**Primary sources**

-   **TED — Tenders Electronic Daily:** EU and EEA procurement planning notices, tender notices, contract awards, direct-award preannouncements, contract modifications, concessions, and utilities procurement
-   **TED Open Data and Search API:** structured procurement data for trend analysis, supplier concentration, incumbent tracking, and award-pattern analysis
-   **EU Funding & Tenders Portal:** EU-funded programs, grants, research calls, consortium activity, and awarded projects
-   National and regional procurement portals for opportunities below EU publication thresholds
-   Public utility, transport, healthcare, municipal, and defence procurement portals

TED contains active procurement notices published in the Supplement to the EU Official Journal and exposes planning, competition, award, and modification notices. Its data is also available for reuse through open datasets and an anonymous search API.

**Signals to watch**

-   Prior Information Notices appearing before a formal tender
-   Contract modifications that expand an incumbent’s scope
-   Recurring framework-agreement winners
-   Common Procurement Vocabulary codes appearing in new buying authorities
-   Evaluation criteria shifting from lowest price toward lifecycle cost, sustainability, cybersecurity, interoperability, or resilience
-   Increased use of negotiated procedures or direct awards
-   Groups of municipalities or utilities buying the same capability
-   Procurement requirements spreading from one Member State into others

**Lead time:** 3–24 months

**Feeds:** TAM/SAM/SOM, account targeting, pricing corridors, partner strategy, product requirements, roadmap timing

### EU Company and Financial Intelligence

**Primary sources**

-   **BRIS — Business Registers Interconnection System**
-   European e-Justice company search
-   National company registries
-   National securities regulators and stock exchanges
-   Annual accounts and ESEF-formatted public-company reports
-   Insolvency registers
-   Beneficial Ownership Register Interconnection System where access is legally available
-   European Commission merger, antitrust, cartel, and state-aid case databases

BRIS connects national business registers and enables company searches across EU and EEA countries. The connected registers may expose legal form, registered seat, representatives, branches, mergers, and annual accounts, although the depth and price of records vary by country.

**Signals to watch**

-   New subsidiaries or branches in target countries
-   Cross-border mergers
-   Changes in directors or legal representatives
-   Subsidiary names revealing a new product or market
-   State-aid awards supporting factory expansion
-   Merger filings exposing market definitions and named competitors
-   Antitrust cases exposing pricing, channel, or exclusivity practices
-   Insolvency activity among distributors, suppliers, or customers

The European Commission’s competition databases allow searches across merger, antitrust, cartel, and state-aid matters, including cases involving manufacturing, energy, transport, telecommunications, and other sectors.

### EU Technical, Research and Standards Intelligence

**Primary sources**

-   European Patent Office and Espacenet
-   EUIPO trademark and design databases
-   **CORDIS** for EU-funded research projects, participants, deliverables, publications, and collaboration networks
-   Horizon Europe projects and consortium announcements
-   CEN, CENELEC, and ETSI work programs
-   Industry-specific standards organizations
-   European Commission research and innovation dashboards
-   Public university and research-institute project repositories

CORDIS provides project participants, funding programs, results, reports, deliverables, publications, datasets, software links, and collaboration visualizations for EU-supported research and innovation programs.

**Signals to watch**

-   Competitors repeatedly appearing in funded consortia
-   Research partners becoming commercial partners
-   Pilot sites indicating likely launch customers
-   Project deliverables becoming product capabilities
-   Competitors chairing standards committees
-   Trademark filings following research-project completion
-   Research consortium members later appearing in acquisitions or partnerships
-   Similar patent, standards, and Horizon-project activity converging around one technology

**Lead time:** 12–48 months for research and standards; 6–18 months for commercialization signals

**Feeds:** technology roadmaps, ecosystem maps, build/buy/partner decisions, long-range threat assessments

### EU Market, Trade and Supply Intelligence

**Primary sources**

-   **Eurostat**
-   Eurostat COMEXT and international trade statistics
-   **Access2Markets**
-   **TARIC**
-   PRODCOM manufacturing statistics
-   European Central Bank data
-   National statistical institutes
-   European Environment Agency industrial and environmental datasets
-   EU customs, trade-defence, anti-dumping, and safeguard proceedings

Eurostat provides official EU statistics, while Access2Markets and TARIC expose trade flows, tariffs, quotas, rules of origin, duties, product requirements, and other import/export conditions.

**Signals to watch**

-   Import volumes growing faster than domestic production
-   New anti-dumping or countervailing investigations
-   Tariff changes altering competitor economics
-   Country-level manufacturing output diverging
-   Competitors changing country of origin or assembly location
-   Sudden movement in product-specific trade codes
-   Local production increasing before a commercial announcement
-   Rules-of-origin changes affecting supplier selection
-   Customs classifications revealing the physical configuration of a product

**Lead time:** 3–24 months

**Feeds:** market sizing, supply-chain strategy, pricing, manufacturing footprint, localization decisions

### EU Regulatory, Certification and Trust Intelligence

**Primary sources**

-   **EUR-Lex**
-   European Commission “Have Your Say” consultations
-   National regulator consultations
-   **NANDO** notified-body database
-   **Safety Gate**
-   European Chemicals Agency databases
-   ENISA and national cybersecurity authorities
-   Sector-specific regulatory databases
-   Product certification, withdrawal, suspension, and recall notices

NANDO identifies notified bodies and the legislation and conformity-assessment tasks for which they are designated. Safety Gate distributes national-authority alerts concerning dangerous non-food products and related corrective measures.

**Signals to watch**

-   Competitors selecting a notified body for a new product category
-   New conformity-assessment capacity appearing in a market
-   Proposed regulations naming technologies or risk classes
-   Public consultation submissions revealing industry positions
-   Product recalls or repeated safety-alert patterns
-   Certification suspensions
-   Competitors lobbying for exceptions or transition periods
-   New cybersecurity or environmental requirements appearing in procurement language

**Lead time:** 6–36 months

**Feeds:** regulatory roadmap, compliance differentiation, trust positioning, product architecture, market-entry timing

## MENA Source Overlay

“MENA” is not one disclosure environment.

The GCC, Levant, and North Africa differ substantially in language, procurement structure, company-data accessibility, state ownership, regulatory publication, and digital maturity.

Complete the Intake Block at the **country level**, not merely “MENA.”

At minimum, specify:

-   GCC, Levant, or North Africa
-   Individual country or countries
-   Arabic, English, or French research requirements
-   Public-sector, state-owned enterprise, or private-sector buyer
-   Local-content requirements
-   Development-bank or sovereign funding involvement

### Regional MENA Sources

Use these before moving into individual country portals.

#### Economic and Market Data

-   **GCC-Stat and its Marsa Data Portal** for GCC population, GDP, trade, energy, tourism, labor, and other regional indicators
-   Arab Development Portal and ESCWA data
-   Arab Monetary Fund datasets
-   World Bank Data and Enterprise Surveys
-   IMF country reports and statistical databases
-   SESRIC statistics for Organisation of Islamic Cooperation countries
-   UN Comtrade and ITC Trade Map
-   National statistics authorities

GCC-Stat provides regional statistics and links to the national statistical agencies of Saudi Arabia, the UAE, Qatar, Oman, Bahrain, and Kuwait. Its data platforms include economic, trade, population, labor, energy, and other indicators.

#### Development-Funded Procurement

-   **Islamic Development Bank project procurement**
-   **World Bank procurement notices**
-   **EBRD Client e-Procurement Portal**
-   **United Nations Global Marketplace**
-   African Development Bank procurement for North Africa
-   European Investment Bank and EU external-action tenders
-   Country-specific development funds and sovereign funds

IsDB publishes project procurement covering goods, works, consulting, and related services across its member countries. EBRD’s ECEPP publishes procurement, prequalification, shortlisting, and award notices for EBRD-financed public-sector projects. UNGM provides procurement notices across participating UN organizations.

**Signals to watch**

-   Development projects entering procurement planning
-   Expressions of interest preceding major tenders
-   Funding approvals identifying future buying authorities
-   Environmental and social assessments revealing equipment requirements
-   Consultant contracts that precede major systems or infrastructure procurement
-   Repeated project-financing partners
-   Procurement packages being split into civil works, equipment, software, and operations
-   Contract awards that establish an incumbent for later phases

| Egypt   | EONEPS — Public e-Procurement System of Egypt | Procurement announcements, supplier registration, catalogs, procurement law, entity notices           |

### MENA Financial and Company Intelligence

**Primary sources**

-   National stock exchanges and securities regulators
-   Prospectus and listed-company disclosure databases
-   Commercial registries
-   Central-bank publications
-   National statistics authorities
-   Sovereign wealth fund reports and portfolio announcements
-   Ministries of finance, economy, investment, industry, energy, and planning
-   Economic-city, industrial-zone, and free-zone registries
-   State-owned enterprise annual reports
-   Public-private partnership project pipelines

For GCC market sizing, begin with GCC-Stat and then move into national sources such as Saudi Arabia’s GASTAT, the UAE’s Federal Competitiveness and Statistics Centre, Qatar’s National Planning Council, Oman’s NCSI, Bahrain’s Information and eGovernment Authority, and Kuwait’s Central Statistical Bureau.

For North Africa, useful official statistical sources include Egypt’s CAPMAS, Morocco’s High Commission for Planning, Tunisia’s National Institute of Statistics, and Algeria’s National Office of Statistics.

**Signals to watch**

-   Sovereign funds establishing a new portfolio company
-   Government ownership being consolidated or reduced
-   State-owned enterprises receiving capital injections
-   Prospectuses naming government projects or anchor customers
-   New industrial licenses
-   Free-zone company registrations
-   Foreign-ownership rules changing
-   Public-private partnership pipelines expanding
-   National development strategies moving from policy language into funded programs
-   Economic diversification spending shifting between sectors

### MENA Ecosystem and Localization Intelligence

In MENA markets, competitive advantage may depend as much on **local presence and state alignment** as product capability.

**Sources**

-   Local-content authorities and procurement rules
-   National industrial-development programs
-   In-country value and localization scorecards
-   Approved vendor lists
-   Distributor and system-integrator registries
-   Chambers of commerce
-   Economic zones and industrial-city directories
-   Sovereign wealth fund portfolios
-   National technology and manufacturing programs
-   Government-backed accelerators
-   Utility, oil and gas, mining, infrastructure, and transport vendor-registration systems

**Signals to watch**

-   Local-content scoring added to tender evaluations
-   Competitors establishing regional headquarters
-   Local assembly or manufacturing announcements
-   Joint ventures with government-related entities
-   New certified distributors
-   Technology-transfer commitments
-   Training academies launched with public institutions
-   Competitors joining national industrial programs
-   Sovereign funds backing a competitor, customer, supplier, or channel partner
-   Procurement eligibility becoming dependent on local registration or classification

Oman’s procurement system, for example, incorporates local-content requirements into tender evaluation, illustrating why local-content intelligence deserves explicit treatment rather than being buried under ordinary procurement analysis.

### MENA Facility and Project Intelligence

**Sources**

-   Industrial-city and economic-zone announcements
-   Environmental permits
-   Utility-connection approvals
-   Construction and municipal permits
-   Port and free-zone tenant announcements
-   Public-private partnership pipelines
-   Ministries of industry, energy, mining, transport, housing, and infrastructure
-   National oil, water, power, rail, and logistics companies
-   Satellite imagery
-   Development-bank environmental and social documents
-   Engineering, procurement, and construction contract awards

**Signals to watch**

-   Land allocation before construction
-   Power or water capacity reserved for a facility
-   Engineering-design contracts preceding equipment procurement
-   Environmental assessment before project approval
-   Port or rail connectivity being expanded
-   New industrial tenants entering an economic zone
-   Local fabrication or assembly requirements
-   Contractor mobilization before an official launch
-   Utilities publishing future-project lists
-   Large projects being divided into successive procurement packages

**Lead time:** 6–36 months

**Feeds:** launch prediction, capacity estimates, market sizing, channel strategy, supply-chain planning, account targeting

### MENA Research Guardrails

-   Search in **Arabic and English** across the GCC and Levant.
-   Search in **Arabic and French** across much of North Africa.
-   Do not assume the English version of a portal contains every notice or document.
-   Treat government strategy announcements as intent until funding, procurement, land, permits, hiring, or contracts corroborate them.
-   Distinguish the government, sovereign fund, state-owned enterprise, regulator, and royal or executive initiative. They may signal different levels of commitment.
-   Watch local-content and vendor-registration requirements. A strong product that cannot qualify to bid is not currently a competitor.
-   Record whether amounts are announced budgets, approved budgets, committed financing, tender values, or awarded contract values.
-   Expect inconsistent identifiers and company-name transliterations. Search Arabic, English, French, abbreviations, former names, and parent-company names.
-   Capture and archive source documents. Regional portals sometimes change structure, replace files, or provide limited historical search.
-   Cross-check headline “megaproject” claims against procurement, contractor awards, development-bank documents, permits, and facility activity.

#### Key Takeaway

In the EU, the challenge is usually **finding the right record inside a rich but fragmented disclosure system**.

In MENA, the challenge is often **separating national ambition from funded, procured, permitted, and operational commitment**.

The categories do not change.

The evidentiary burden does.
