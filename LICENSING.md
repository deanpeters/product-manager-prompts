## What you can do freely (and are encouraged to)

- **Use** any prompt, generator, workshop, investigation, or loop
  recipe for your own learning and product practice
- **Adapt** them — remix, translate, restructure, improve
- **Share** them and your adaptations with your team, community,
  class, or meetup

...as long as you do two things:

1. **Attribute**: credit Dean Peters and link back to this
   repository. One line is fine:
   > Adapted from [product-manager-prompts](https://github.com/deanpeters/product-manager-prompts)

2. **ShareAlike**: anything you build *from* this material and

## What requires expressed written permission

**Any commercial use requires expressed written permission from
Dean Peters.** That includes, without limitation:

- Selling this library or any adaptation of it
- Bundling it into a paid product, app, or service
- Using it as material for paid training, courses, workshops, or
  consulting deliverables
- Repackaging it behind a paywall, login, or lead-capture wall

Wanting to do commercial work with this material is not a problem —
it just starts with a conversation. Open an issue on this
repository or contact Dean Peters (Productside.com) and ask.
Permission, when granted, will be explicit and in writing.

## Attribution inside the library

Individual prompts carry their own `Attribution` field crediting
the frameworks and thinkers that inspired them (Porter, Torres,
Gothelf, Klein, and others). Those frameworks remain their
creators' ideas; the prompt text here is original, clean-room
expression by Dean Peters. Keep those attribution fields intact
when you adapt a prompt — they are part of the attribution this
license requires.
